Shigeru Miyamoto


Shigeru Miyamoto-born on November 16, 1952, in Kyoto, Japan-is a Japanese video game designer and producer. Serving currently as the co-Representative Director of Nintendo, he is credited for creating the best-selling video games of all time, such as Donkey Kong, Mario Bros, and The Legend of Zelda

.

1977 - 1984 : Arcade Beginnings

Miyamoto joined Nintendo when his father, Iijake Miyamoto, arranged him an interview with the company's president Hiroshi Yamauchi. In the interview, Shigeru Miyamoto presented his toy creations and was hired as the company's first artist. He then helped Nintendo the arcade game, "Sheriff", as well as the game, "Radar Scope". Miyamoto also helped develop the game, "Donkey Kong", using "Beauty and the Beast and "King Kong" as influences.

1985 - 1989 : NES

During this time, the Nintendo Entertainment System, as it was called in America, was released. At the same time, Miyamoto made two of world's most known video games: Super Mario Bros and The Legend of Zelda. He then worked on the sequels of each of those games, as well as, on other games for the NES, such as Ice Climber, Kid Icarius, Excitebike, and Devil World.
